Welcome to the rich history and knowledge of TechCon

The TechCon Story

TechCon began with a bold mission: to revolutionize fluid dispensing in high-precision industries like aerospace, defense, and medical device manufacturing. What started as a small team of engineers has evolved into a global leader in dispensing technology. From our earliest innovations to today's advanced automated systems, TechCon has stayed true to its core values — precision, reliability, and adaptability. Our solutions help manufacturers achieve exceptional accuracy, reduce waste, and meet the most demanding production standards. As we look to the future, we`re pushing boundaries with smart systems, AI integration, and sustainable design — powering the next generation of global innovation.

Our Mission at TechCon

At TechCon, our mission is to ignite innovation, foster collaboration, and drive progress in high-impact industries across the globe. We bring together engineers, innovators, researchers, and industry leaders to explore transformative technologies and solutions that shape the future. Guided by a commitment to excellence, sustainability, and knowledge-sharing, TechCon serves as a platform for breakthrough ideas, meaningful partnerships, and practical solutions to real-world challenges. We believe that by uniting diverse minds under one roof, we can unlock new possibilities and accelerate technological advancement for the benefit of all.

Notable Speakers From Previous Years

Chris Barton

Chris Barton is an American tech entrepreneur, inventor, investor, and keynote speaker. He founded Shazam, a music identification company, and was its first CEO.

Peter H. Diamandis

Peter H. Diamandis is an American engineer, physician, and entrepreneur. He is best known as the founder and chairman of the XPRIZE Foundation, and the cofounder and executive chairman of Singularity University

Amy Webb

Amy Lynn Webb is an American futurist, author and founder and CEO of the Future Today Strategy Group. She is an adjunct assistant professor at New York University's Stern School of Business, a nonresident senior fellow at Atlantic Council, and was a 2014-15 Visiting Nieman Fellow at Harvard University
